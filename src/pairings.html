The Pairing

What to Drink with Everything in Kitsilano

The spots we eat at ourselves, paired with the bottles we'd actually bring. Curated by Chris Reid, 20 years on the floor.

Heading out to dinner in Kits, or picking up takeout on your way home? Stop in on the way and we'll send you off with the right bottle. The shop is at 2752 W Broadway, open daily 10am to 11pm.

The Restaurant Guide

Pairings by Spot

Most of these places are corkage-friendly. Call ahead to confirm and check the fee. Eight bucks for a great bottle you bring yourself is one of the best deals in the city.

Maenam

Modern Thai  ·  1938 W 4th Ave

Angus An's flagship. Bold Thai flavours, serious heat, refined plating. The wine list is excellent, but if you're bringing your own, you want aromatics and a little sweetness to ride the spice.

Bring These

  • Alsace Riesling Off-dry, floral, with the acidity to cut through coconut and the residual sugar to tame chilli. The classic Thai pairing.
  • Pétillant Naturel Bubbles are an underrated move with spicy food. Lifts everything on the plate, never fights it.
  • Beaujolais Villages If you want a red, this is it. Light, fruity, low-tannin Gamay handles Thai food better than anything heavy ever will.

AnnaLena

Modern Pacific Northwest  ·  1809 W 1st Ave

Tasting menu only, and worth every penny. Mike Robbins's cooking is precise and local-forward. The wine pairings are exceptional, but if you're bringing one for the table before or after, here's where to start.

Bring These

  • Grower Champagne This is a place to celebrate. Grower Champagne is more interesting than the big houses and pairs with everything Mike sends out.
  • BC Pinot Noir (Okanagan) If the menu is leaning into BC ingredients, lean into BC wine. The Okanagan is making Pinot that belongs in this room.
  • Village Burgundy The safe-but-stunning move. A good village Burgundy never embarrasses anyone, especially next to refined Pacific Northwest cooking.

Au Comptoir

French Bistro  ·  2278 W 4th Ave

The closest thing to a real Parisian bistro on this side of the country. Steak frites, escargot, duck confit. The food is French, so the wine should be French.

Bring These

  • Bernard Baudry Chinon Loire Cabernet Franc done right. He started with a vineyard so small he called it a handkerchief. Read more on the Baudry post.
  • Côtes du Rhône Rouge Grenache-led, generous, made for bistro food. France has been drinking this with steak frites for centuries.
  • Beaujolais Villages Light Gamay with duck. With pork rillettes. With anything. The cheapest way to drink like a Parisian.

Folke

Plant-Based, Natural Wine  ·  2585 W Broadway

Literally a block from the shop. Plant-forward menu, deeply natural-wine-leaning bar. They have a great list of their own, but if you're bringing something, lean low-intervention.

Bring These

  • Natural Red Selection Low-intervention, minimal sulphites, maximum character. Come in and we'll find one that matches the kitchen's energy.
  • Pétillant Naturel Ancient method, lively bubbles, zero pretension. The wine world's most honest fizz, and Folke's natural angle suits it perfectly.
  • Beaujolais Villages Plant-forward food needs light, juicy reds. Gamay is the answer almost every time.

The Painted Ship

PNW · Mexican · Asian Share Plates  ·  2884 W Broadway

A few doors down Broadway from us. Michael Brennan's newest concept with the Mum's the Word crew. Vintage 60s/70s room, share-plate menu that bounces from PNW to Mexican to Asian, live music and DJs late. A genuinely happening room. Bring something that can keep up.

Bring These

  • A Sunday in August (BC) Low-intervention, lively, a little funky. The right bottle for a room with this much energy and a menu that won't sit still.
  • Reposado Mezcal When the menu leans into the Mexican thread, this is the move. Smoky, slow-sipping, made for share plates. Ask us at the shop.
  • Beaujolais Villages When the menu jumps from cuisine to cuisine, light Gamay survives all of it. The most versatile red on the shelf.

Take-Out & Delivery

Pairings for What You're Ordering

Most nights you're not going out. You're ordering. Here's what to open with what's at the door.

Pizza Friday

Wood-fired, pepperoni, the lot

  • Barbera d'AstiItaly's pizza wine. High acid, low tannin, made for tomato sauce.
  • Beaujolais VillagesIf pepperoni is involved, Gamay never lets you down.
  • Côtes du Rhône RougeBigger, rustic, great with anything aggressively cheesy.

Sushi Night

Tojo's, Sushi Mura, Miku takeaway

  • Pétillant NaturelBubbles with sushi is criminally underrated.
  • Attems Sauvignon BlancCrisp, clean, salt-forward. Disappears into the meal in the best way.
  • Junmai SakeThe obvious answer is the right one. We have a curated selection.

Burger & Fries

Save On Meats, Romer's, the patio at Local

  • Argentine MalbecThe grape France let go and Argentina made famous. Built for a burger.
  • BC Craft IPAA cold can is sometimes the only right answer. We have the wall for it.
  • Willamette Pinot NoirIf the burger is at a real restaurant, this is the move.

Indian & Curry Night

Vij's, Rangoli, neighbourhood spots

  • Off-Dry RieslingThe textbook spice pairing. Sweet just enough to tame heat, dry enough to feel serious.
  • Pétillant NaturelBubbles cut through ghee and chilli oil better than anything still.
  • Beaujolais VillagesThe one red that survives a vindaloo. Light, juicy, low-tannin Gamay.

Cheese Board & Charcuterie

Date night in

  • Bernard Baudry ChinonLoire Cab Franc with hard cheese is one of the great moves of all time.
  • Grower ChampagneIf the board has anything funky or blue, the bubbles save you every time.
  • Tawny PortThe end-of-night move. Stilton, walnuts, a small pour. Devastating in the right way.

Movie Night, No Cooking

Popcorn, leftover Thai, whatever's in the freezer

  • BC Craft TallboysThe wall of cans exists for exactly this. Local, cold, easy.
  • A Good Negroni RTDPre-batched Negronis from craft producers. One of the fastest-growing categories in the shop, and for good reason.
  • Beaujolais VillagesLight enough to drink without thinking. Good enough to make the night feel like something.
